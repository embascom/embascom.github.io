Assignment 8: Final Project!

For my final project, I created various household itemst that my partner and I needed after moving into our new apartment through a combination of my own designs and designs from Thingiverse.

Final Project Specifications

For my final project, I had proposed to create the following:

  • 1× bar soap holder
  • 1x drain catch
  • 1× liquid dish soap holder
  • 2× q-tip holders
  • 2× cotton round holders
  • 1× toothbrush holder

With the stretch goals of the following:

  • Various hooks for throughout the apartment
  • Various drawers dividers for the kitchen and bathroom
  • Various cat toys for my little babies

In total, for my final project, I created:
  • 2× bar soap holder
  • 1x drain catch
  • 1× liquid dish soap holder
  • 1× q-tip holders
  • 1× cotton round holders
  • 1× toothbrush holder
  • 1x toothpaste squeezer
  • 1x shelf
  • 1x catch-all dish

In summary, I completed all of my initally proposed pieces, with the exception of the duplicate q-tip and cotton round holders, and also created 2.5 extra pieces (the catch-all dish came from an error in printing so doesn't really count).

Final Pieces

Sponge/Bar Soap Holder

For this piece, I cast a squiggly sponge holder in plaster, ended up with what looked like it should be from a roman ruin and ended up 3D printing it to make it pretty.

Files

  • Thingiverse File Link
  • Rhino Files - Mold and Mold Frame
  • Cura Files - Sponge Holder, Mold, and Mold Frame

Cura Settings

Here are the settings I changed when printing this piece (all other settings were left to default):

  • Stnadard Quality
  • Wall Thickness - 1.0 mm
  • Top/Bottom Thickness - 1.0 mm
  • Infill Denisty - 20.0%
  • Infill Pattern - Cross 3D
  • Printing Temperature - 200.0 C
  • Build Plate Temperature - 60.0 C
  • Initial Layer Speed - 5.0 mm/s
  • Initial Layer Print Speed - 15.0 mm/s

Process

I first started by creating the positive for the silicone mold in Rhino. I then created a four piece frame that I had planned on using to contain the silicone when casting it. This became an issue because I did not make the frame pieces big enough but also only printed one set even though there are two sides to the frame... This resulted in me making another frame out of cardboard which shockingly worked. Once silicone had cured, I was peeling it off of the 3D printed positive and realized that I had most definitely made the pour spout too small and that my pieces keeping the two sides of the mold together were too small. I perservered and cast it anyway, wrapping the piece in duck tape which barely worked. I did get most of the piece to set but did have to break off some imperfect parts. I was not satisfied with this so I ended up 3D printing the original Thingiverse piece and am much happier with this.

  • Infill Pattern - Cross 3D
  • Printing Temperature - 200.0 C
  • Build Plate Temperature - 60.0 C
  • Initial Layer Speed - 5.0 mm/s
  • Initial Layer Print Speed - 15.0 mm/s
  • Support Structure - Tree
  • Support Overhang Angle - 65.0

Process

For this print, I scaled up the original creator's design to make the piece bigger as for some reason I get really frustrated with how small most q-tip holders are. I really liked this three-piece design and wanted to leverage this to use my different color filaments. Upon printing the top lid in red, I realized that for some reason my printer really does not like this filament, and I had to slow the intial layer print down to 5 mm/s (which I ended up using for all my prints moving forward) and manually adjust the bed level while the inital print was running. No amount of bed level beforehand seems to help but once I got the printer calibrated after about 7 or 8 failed prints, it has been working fairly well with mild warping.

For this piece, I used this Thingiverse file by emmett.

Here are the settings I changed when printing this piece (all other settings were left to default):

  • Low Quality
  • Wall Thickness - 1.0 mm
  • Top/Bottom Thickness - 1.0 mm
  • Infill Denisty - 20.0%
  • Infill Pattern - Cross 3D
  • Printing Temperature - 200.0 C

  • Initial Layer Speed - 5.0 mm/s
  • Initial Layer Print Speed - 15.0 mm/s
  • Support Structure - Tree
  • Support Overhang Angle - 65.0

Process

This piece was extremely frustrating for me. Because it has a moving part, I initally printed this piece on Super Quality, but I ran into two major issues on this initial print; first, I had put the piece too close to the edge on the print plate, resulting in a big chunk of it being missing, and second, I was in denial about the warping issue with my prints, so I just let it run. This resulted in me not being able to get the roter in all the way, rendering the piece useless. I then reprined on low quality which worked really well because it gives the pieces a lot more flexibility.
